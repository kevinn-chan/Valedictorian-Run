# Product

## Register

product

## Platform

web

## Users

Two allowlisted university students (currently one: Kevin), revising for exams with their own
course materials — lecture slide PDFs, notes, tutorial cheatsheets. Context of use: long study
sessions under exam pressure, often late, often tired. The job to be done: turn a pile of course
files into something they can actually learn from — summaries, a plan, flashcards, and answers
they can trust. No other audience; this is a private tool, not a growth product.

## Product Purpose

Valedictorian Run turns a dropped-in corpus of course files into a compiled study system: a
browsable topic wiki, digestible summaries, a targeted learning plan, spaced-repetition cue
cards, and a Q&A chat that answers only from the student's own materials with page-level
citations. Success looks like: a student opens it the week before an exam, and every hour spent
inside it is an hour of real revision — no tab-switching to raw PDFs, no wondering whether an
answer is hallucinated.

## Positioning

Every summary, flashcard, and answer comes from your own course materials — with the page to
prove it.

## Brand Personality

Calm library crossed with a warm personal notebook. Quiet, focused, and spacious like a reading
room — studying is stressful and the tool must not add to it — but personal and gently
encouraging like a well-kept notebook, not sterile. Three words: calm, warm, trustworthy.
Voice: a knowledgeable study partner who has read all your notes; plain sentences, no hype,
small moments of warmth (progress acknowledged quietly, not celebrated with confetti).

## Anti-references

- Corporate SaaS dashboard: hero metrics, purple gradients, identical card grids, growth-tool
  coldness.
- ChatGPT clone: the product must not read as a bare chatbot wrapper — the student's corpus,
  not the chat box, is the star of every screen.
- Academic brutalism: the cold, dense, 90s-university-portal aesthetic; institutional gray
  tables and cramped type.

## Design Principles

1. **The corpus is the star.** Every screen anchors on the student's own materials — their
   files, their topics, their pages. AI output is always presented as a view over the corpus,
   never as free-floating machine text.
2. **Trust through provenance.** Every claim carries a `[file p.N]` citation that opens the
   real page. Nothing unsourced; refusal ("not in your materials") is a feature, shown calmly.
3. **Calm under pressure.** The user is stressed by exams, not by us: generous spacing,
   unhurried motion, no urgency mechanics, no red badges screaming for attention.
4. **Warmth without noise.** Personality lives in typography, copy, and small touches — never
   in decoration that competes with study content.
5. **Built for two.** No growth machinery, no onboarding funnels; the app may be personal and
   direct (first names, remembered preferences) because every user is known.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Baseline defaults (not user-specified — refine anytime): WCAG AA contrast throughout, full
keyboard operability for the flashcard review flow, and `prefers-reduced-motion` respected on
every animation.
